During my internship and learning journey with AWS, I have synthesized my knowledge into technical sharing articles (Blogs). Writing blogs not only helps reinforce the theoretical concepts I have learned but also allows me to share practical perspectives on system architecture with the community. 

Below is the list of blogs I have completed and published:

### [Blog 1 - EKS Pod Identity and Session Policies: Centralized Authorization Architecture on Kubernetes](3.1-Blog1/)
This article analyzes how to solve the complex permission management challenge in large-scale Kubernetes environments. By combining the power of Amazon EKS Pod Identity and Session Policies, it guides the transition from the traditional, cumbersome IRSA mechanism to a streamlined, fully automated 3-tier security architecture. This ensures the "least privilege" principle is strictly followed without overloading the system with excessive IAM Roles.

### [Blog 2 - AWS Well-Architected Framework: The Continuously Evolving Cloud Design Mindset](3.2-Blog2/)
Based on the latest updates from AWS, this post delivers a clear message: WAF is not a static checklist, but a living playbook. The content explores the art of trade-offs among the 6 core pillars, how to break down communication silos between Engineering, Finance, and Executive teams, and affirms that architecture review must be a continuous journey throughout the lifecycle of a Cloud system.

### [Blog 3 - Protecting Sensitive Data with Data Masking in Amazon RDS for Oracle](3.3-Blog3/)
This post shares a practical perspective on the importance of data security when creating Development or UAT environments from Production data. By analyzing an automated workflow combining Amazon EventBridge, Step Functions, Systems Manager, and Secrets Manager, the article demonstrates how to mask sensitive customer information, enabling development and testing teams to work safely without risking data leaks.
